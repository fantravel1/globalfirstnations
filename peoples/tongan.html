Who Are the Tongans?

Tongans are the indigenous Polynesian people of the Kingdom of Tonga. Tonga is unique as the only remaining indigenous monarchy in Polynesia and one of the few Pacific nations never formally colonized. Tongans are renowned for their strong cultural identity, royal traditions, seafaring heritage, and the centrality of family and Christian faith.

Culture & Traditions

Polynesian Monarchy

The Tongan monarchy is central to national identity. The constitutional system balances traditional royal authority with modern democracy. Nobles hold hereditary titles, and commoners show respect through traditional protocols.

Kava Ceremony

Kava ceremonies (taumafa kava) mark important occasions with specific protocols for preparation and serving based on social rank. This ancient tradition binds community and honors hierarchy.

Tapa & Traditional Arts

Tapa cloth making (ngatu) is a highly developed art where women beat mulberry bark and paint intricate geometric designs. These beautiful textiles mark important occasions and represent cultural continuity.
