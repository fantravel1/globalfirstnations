Asante Empire

The Asante Empire (1670-1902) was one of West Africa's most powerful states, unified under the Golden Stool (Sika Dwa Kofi)â€”believed to have descended from the sky, containing the soul of the Asante nation. The empire controlled gold trade, developed sophisticated bureaucracy, and fielded powerful armies. The Asantehene (king) ruled from Kumasi, the capital. Four Anglo-Asante wars (1824-1900) demonstrated Asante military capability; only after the War of the Golden Stool (1900) did Britain fully control Asante territory. The Asante kingdom continues today as a constitutional monarchy within Ghana; the current Asantehene, Osei Tutu II, wields significant traditional authority.
