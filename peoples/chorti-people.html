Who Are the Ch'orti'?

The Ch'orti' Maya are an indigenous people of eastern Guatemala, western Honduras, and parts of El Salvador, numbering approximately 30,000-60,000. They speak Ch'orti', a Mayan language descended from Classic Maya and closely related to the language of ancient Cop√°n's inscriptions. The language is endangered, with most speakers in Guatemala and very few remaining in Honduras. The Ch'orti' territory surrounds Cop√°n, one of the great Classic Maya cities, making them the likely descendants of its ancient inhabitants. They represent the southernmost Maya people and a living connection to Classic Maya civilization.

Land Struggles in Honduras

The Ch'orti' of Honduras face extreme poverty and land dispossession. The "Corredor Seco" (Dry Corridor) of eastern Honduras, their homeland, suffers drought and food insecurity. Land concentration by cattle ranchers has displaced Ch'orti' communities. Indigenous organizations fight for land redistribution and recognition. The Honduran Ch'orti' have fewer speakers and weaker cultural continuity than their Guatemalan relatives, having faced more complete assimilation pressure.
