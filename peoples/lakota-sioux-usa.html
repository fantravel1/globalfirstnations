Buffalo Culture

Lakota life centered on the buffalo (tatanka). Hunting provided food, clothing, shelter (tipi covers), tools, and spiritual meaning. The Sun Dance, Lakota's most sacred ceremony, gave thanks and sought visions. Nomadic bands followed buffalo across the northern plains; the horse (arriving c. 1750) transformed hunting and warfare. Buffalo society created warrior culturesâ€”counting coup, hunting prowess, and vision quests defined masculine achievement. US policy deliberately destroyed buffalo herds (from millions to near-extinction) to subjugate Plains tribesâ€”an environmental and cultural genocide. Without buffalo, Lakota were forced onto reservations.
