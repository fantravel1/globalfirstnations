Introduction

The Basque people are an indigenous ethnic group inhabiting the Basque Country region straddling northern Spain and southwestern France. Speaking Euskara, a language unrelated to any other known language (predating Indo-European arrival in Europe), the Basques represent one of Europe's oldest cultures. Known for strong cultural identity, distinctive traditions including pelota (ball game), and world-renowned cuisine, Basques have maintained cultural continuity despite centuries of external pressures.
