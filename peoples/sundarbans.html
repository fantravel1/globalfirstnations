Sundarbans Communities

Sundarbans communities (millions) inhabit the world's largest mangrove forest spanning Bangladesh/India‚Äîa tidal wetland where Royal Bengal tigers kill 50-100 people annually. They endure tiger predation while fishing/honey collecting, cyclones, rising seas, saltwater intrusion, extreme poverty, and amphibious existence.
