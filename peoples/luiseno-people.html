Traditional Lifeways

Traditional Luise√±o territory extended from the Pacific coast to the inland valleys and mountains, encompassing diverse ecosystems that supported a varied subsistence economy. Acorns from coast live oaks were the dietary staple, processed into flour using mortars and pestles, many of which remain visible as bedrock mortars at village sites. Coastal groups harvested shellfish and sea mammals; inland groups hunted deer, rabbits, and small game. Plant foods including seeds, berries, roots, and agave supplemented the diet. Villages were politically autonomous, each with hereditary leadership, but participated in regional ceremonial and exchange networks. The Chinigchinich religion, centered on puberty initiations and the use of toloache (jimsonweed), spread among the Luise√±o and neighboring groups in the centuries before contact.
