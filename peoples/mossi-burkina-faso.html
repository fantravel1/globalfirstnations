Mossi Kingdoms

The Mossi Kingdoms (Ouagadougou, Yatenga, Tenkodogo, and others) were among West Africa's most powerful and enduring states. Founded by horseback warriors who conquered indigenous populations and established a ruling dynasty, the kingdoms developed sophisticated administration, cavalry armies, and strong monarchical tradition. Uniquely, they resisted Islamizationâ€”maintaining traditional religion and practices while surrounding peoples converted. This resistance preserved distinct Mossi identity. French conquest (1896-1897) ended political independence but not traditional authority. The Mogho Naaba of Ouagadougou continues performing weekly ceremonies and holds significant moral authority in modern Burkina Faso.
