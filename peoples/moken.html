Who Are the Moken?

The Moken are seafaring people of the Andaman Sea, known as 'Sea Gypsies.' They have traditionally lived a nomadic existence aboard their kabang boats, spending most of their lives at sea. They are extraordinary free divers with the remarkable ability to see clearly underwater - an adaptation developed through generations of marine life.

Culture & Way of Life

Marine Adaptations

Moken possess remarkable physical and cultural adaptations to marine life. They can dive to 20 meters and stay submerged for several minutes. Children develop the ability to constrict pupils and change lens shape to see clearly underwater. They have exceptional knowledge of fish behavior, coral reefs, tides, and currents.

Traditional Boats

Traditional kabang boats served as floating homes for entire families. Built from local wood without nails, families cooked, slept, and raised children aboard. Today, few kabang remain as most Moken have been forced to settle on land.

Contemporary Challenges

Traditional sea nomadism has largely ended. Most Moken now live in settled communities, facing statelessness, poverty, and cultural loss. However, they work to preserve their heritage through teaching traditional knowledge, language preservation, and advocating for maritime rights.
