Kalasha People

The Kalasha (3,000-4,000) inhabit three remote valleys in Pakistan's Hindu Kush mountains at 1,500-3,000 meters. They maintain pre-Islamic polytheistic religion, colorful traditional dress, and unique customs isolated for centuries. Surviving harsh winters, limited agricultural land, and cultural pressures, they represent one of South Asia's last non-Muslim indigenous groups in an extreme mountain environment.
