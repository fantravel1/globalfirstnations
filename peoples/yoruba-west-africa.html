City-States and History

Yoruba civilization produced remarkable urban centers. Ile-Ife, considered the spiritual origin of the Yoruba people and all humanity in their cosmology, created stunning bronze and terracotta heads (12th-15th centuries) rivaling Renaissance art. The Oyo Empire (15th-19th centuries) became West Africa's largest pre-colonial state, with cavalry armies and sophisticated administration. Other city-statesâ€”Ibadan, Abeokuta, Ijebuâ€”had distinct characters. British colonization (completed 1893) ended political independence but not cultural vitality. This urban heritage distinguishes Yoruba from many African peoples; contemporary Lagos, Nigeria's megacity, continues the urban tradition.

Contemporary Yoruba

Modern Yoruba play major roles in Nigerian politics, business, and culture. Lagos, predominantly Yoruba, is Africa's largest city and Nigeria's economic engine. Yoruba politicians have held Nigeria's highest offices; Yoruba entrepreneurs lead major corporations.
