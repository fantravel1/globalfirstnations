300 Years Unconquered

The Mapuche ('people of the land' in Mapudungun) are an indigenous people inhabiting south-central Chile and southwestern Argentina. With a population of approximately 1.7 million, they represent Chile's largest indigenous group and maintain one of the most remarkable records of resistance in indigenous history‚Äîsuccessfully defending their territory against both the Inca Empire and Spanish conquistadors for over 300 years. The Mapuche preserved their autonomy until the late 19th century, when Chilean and Argentine military campaigns finally incorporated Mapuche territories into modern nation-states.
