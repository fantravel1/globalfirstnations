Who Are the Siona?

The Siona are an indigenous Amazonian people numbering approximately 2,000, split between Colombia (~1,200) and Ecuador (~800) along the Putumayo River and its tributaries. They speak Siona, a Western Tucanoan language closely related to Secoya. The Siona were historically among the most accomplished yag√© (ayahuasca) practitioners in the Amazon; their shamanic traditions influenced global understanding of this plant medicine. Their territory spans the Colombia-Ecuador border, a region affected by conflict, drug trafficking, and oil development.
